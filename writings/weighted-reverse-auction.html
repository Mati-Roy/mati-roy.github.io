Weighted reverse auction

a fair way to assign shared tasks

Context

Situation

In some situations a group of 2+ people need a task to be done.

Assumption

Group of honest people (otherwise this breaks down in some ways).

Reverse auction

1) Bid. Everyone writes a price for which they would be indifferent between doing the task or paying someone else to do it.

2) Select person. The person with the lowest bid does the task.

3) Select price. Take the lowest bid.

Details: This incentivizes to bid one’s true value, like in a Vickrey auction.

Alternative pricing: To share the profit, the other people pay the person in (2) the amount of money of the second lowest bid.

This method assumes everyone will accomplish the task equally well. See next section if this assumption is false or undetermined.

Weighted reverse auction

1) Bid. People write a price for which they would be indifferent between doing the task or paying one of their clone to do it (if that was an option).

2) Evaluate capacity. People attribute number to everyone representing the fraction of the price they would be ready to pay the best person to do the task according to them.

Details: One can be ready to pay more someone if they are: trustworthy, reliable, competent, or otherwise in a better position to accomplish the task, or have indirect reasons why they would be a valuable person to accomplish the task (ex.: train them). Oneself rates oneself as 1 as defined by (1).

Example: If you’d be ready to pay someone else 50% less because you think they’ll do a worse job then the best person would, then you assign them a weight of 0.5. Implied: Put 0 for someone if you wouldn’t want them to do the job even for 1 cent.

Variant: The weight can also be anonymized.

Extension: If someone opts out of the bidding, their bid is set at infinity, and their weight not counted.

3) Aggregate competence evaluation. Everyone averages out the number they received from everyone else.

Alternatives: multiply the numbers; take the logarithmic average; take the median

4) Calculate competence-adjusted bid. Everyone divides their price (1) by the average people valued them at (3).

5) Select person. The person with the lowest number at (4) does the task.

Details: If there’s a tie, the person can randomly be selected.

6) Select price. Take the lowest bid

Details: This also incentivizes to bid one’s true value, like in a Vickrey auction

Alternative pricing: To share the profit, the other people pay the person in (5) the amount of money of the second lowest bid.

7) Split the cost. Split the cost equally between all housemate, including among the person that will do the task.

Addendum: Someone from outside the group could also bid, but the money will be paid by the people inside the group in either case.

There are some specific cases where some people might disagree with the split, but I think this would happen rarely, and when it does, a combination of people might need to do the task.

Example: some people might need to pay someone more than they would have been ready to be paid, and personally disagree that the person is more competent.

Addendum: If someone in a group really doesn’t benefit from a task, they can opt-out. Otherwise, we should try to reach consensus on task selection.

Alternative: everyone bids, then people vote for someone

Addendum

We first tried with the weight for oneself being 1 and adjusted other’s weights accordingly, but this overweight some people’s preferences.

Norm: benefit of the doubt; put 1 to new housemates; (except for responsibility that are public facing, like finding new housemates, in which case it’s better if a long term tenant aware with the house does it).

Norm: fund should be kept in a separate account or the account of a trusted tenant; but the rest of the finance responsibility can be done by anyone.

Alternative: If your intentional community prefers for everyone to contribute an equal amount of direct energy (as oppose of energy or funding), you can use a variant. Such a variant, that isn’t quality-weighted, can be found on: https://www.splitit.com/ (related: Assignment problem).

Extension: If two people want to work on a task, the two lowest bid can do the task at the second lowest bid.

Edge cases

Consider people A, B, C.

Price A B C

A 10$ 1 0.1 1

B 10$ 0.1 1 1

C 1000$ 1 1 1

In this case, everyone would prefer if A and B did the task together, so the method fails.

Current patch: In general, if someone has no one else to outsource the task to that is less expensive than them, but haven’t been selected to do the task, then they can join the other person in doing the task (and presumably avoid paying them to do the task).
